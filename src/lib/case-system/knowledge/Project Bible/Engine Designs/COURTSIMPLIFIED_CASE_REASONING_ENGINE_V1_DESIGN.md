# COURTSIMPLIFIED CASE REASONING ENGINE V1

STATUS: DESIGN PHASE

OWNER:
CourtSimplified Core Intelligence Layer

PURPOSE

The Case Reasoning Engine is responsible for converting raw user facts into structured litigation reasoning.

This engine sits above intake and evidence collection and below strategy generation.

It does not provide legal advice.

It performs legal reasoning analysis.

---

PRIMARY OBJECTIVES

1. Detect facts

2. Detect legal issues

3. Detect claims

4. Detect defenses

5. Detect procedural concerns

6. Detect evidentiary gaps

7. Detect contradictions

8. Detect burden failures

9. Detect judicial concerns

10. Generate reasoning pathways

---

INPUTS

Case Narrative

Evidence Packages

Timeline Data

Procedural Status

Authority Registry

Annual Practice Registry

MasterCaseSchema

---

OUTPUTS

Fact Findings

Issue Findings

Claim Findings

Defense Findings

Proof Findings

Burden Findings

Judicial Concern Findings

Missing Evidence Findings

Strategy Findings

Recommended Next Actions

---

CORE REASONING LAYERS

Layer 1

Fact Detection

Questions:

What happened?

Who acted?

When?

Where?

Against whom?

---

Layer 2

Issue Detection

Questions:

What legal problems exist?

What procedural problems exist?

What evidentiary problems exist?

---

Layer 3

Claim Detection

Questions:

What claims are available?

What causes of action may exist?

What relief may be available?

---

Layer 4

Defense Detection

Questions:

What defenses exist?

What responses are likely?

What procedural objections may occur?

---

Layer 5

Proof Analysis

Questions:

What must be proven?

What evidence supports each element?

What evidence is missing?

---

Layer 6

Burden Analysis

Questions:

Who carries the burden?

Has the burden been satisfied?

What elements remain unsupported?

---

Layer 7

Judicial Concern Analysis

Questions:

What concerns would a judge likely have?

What credibility concerns exist?

What procedural concerns exist?

What evidentiary concerns exist?

---

Layer 8

Contradiction Analysis

Questions:

What facts conflict?

What evidence conflicts?

What testimony conflicts?

What procedural positions conflict?

---

Layer 9

Strategy Analysis

Questions:

What strengthens the case?

What weakens the case?

What should be done next?

---

LONG TERM GOAL

The Case Reasoning Engine becomes the central cognitive engine for CourtSimplified.

All future intelligence systems connect to this engine.

No duplicate reasoning engines should be created.

This engine becomes the single source of truth for litigation reasoning.
