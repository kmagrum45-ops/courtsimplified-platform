# COURTSIMPLIFIED EVIDENCE INTELLIGENCE ENGINE V1

STATUS: DESIGN PHASE

OWNER

CourtSimplified Cognitive Layer

PURPOSE

The Evidence Intelligence Engine evaluates, organizes, analyzes, and connects evidence within a case.

The purpose is not merely to store evidence.

The purpose is to understand:

- what evidence exists
- what evidence proves
- how strong evidence is
- what evidence conflicts
- what evidence is missing
- what evidence creates risk

This engine serves as the central evidence analysis system for CourtSimplified.

---

WHY THIS ENGINE EXISTS

Many litigants collect evidence without understanding:

- why it matters
- what it proves
- whether it is admissible
- whether it is strong
- whether it is corroborated
- whether it creates risk

This engine converts evidence into litigation intelligence.

---

PRIMARY QUESTIONS

What evidence exists?

What facts does the evidence support?

What facts does the evidence contradict?

How strong is the evidence?

How reliable is the evidence?

How credible is the evidence?

How persuasive is the evidence?

What evidence is missing?

What evidence should be obtained next?

EVIDENCE RELIABILITY MODEL

Questions:

Was the evidence created contemporaneously?

Is the source identifiable?

Can authenticity be verified?

Has the evidence been altered?

Is independent verification available?

---

CORROBORATION ANALYSIS

Questions:

Is the evidence supported elsewhere?

Do multiple sources support the same fact?

Does witness evidence support documentary evidence?

Does documentary evidence support testimony?

LONG TERM GOAL

The Evidence Intelligence Engine becomes the primary evidence analysis system for CourtSimplified.

It should help users understand:

- what evidence they possess
- what evidence matters
- what evidence is weak
- what evidence is strong
- what evidence is missing
- what evidence supports their position
- what evidence undermines their position

No duplicate evidence-analysis systems should be created.

This engine becomes the single source of truth for evidence intelligence throughout CourtSimplified.
